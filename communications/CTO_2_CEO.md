## Executive Summary
**You are correct.** From a physics and manufacturing standpoint, your proposed 4 parameters cover the primary drivers of swelling. Restraining the scope to these "Process Levers" significantly reduces project risk and time-to-market.

## Technical Validation of Your 4 Parameters

1.  **Binder Type (Chemistry):** **CRITICAL.** This is the hydrophilic "sponge." Changing from Phenolic to Pitch or Furan is the single most effective chemical switch to stop water uptake.
2.  **Pressing Pressure (Density):** **CRITICAL.** Higher pressure = higher density = lower open porosity volume. This mechanically removes the "space" for water.
3.  **Carbonization Temperature (Structure):** **HIGHLY RELEVANT.** Higher temps (>900°C) shrink the binder coke and close micropores. It also increases crystallinity, which is inherently more hydrophobic.
4.  **Coating/Impregnation (Sealing):** **CRITICAL.** A silane or oil post-dip is the cheapest, fastest way to create a hydrophobic barrier without changing the bulk material.

## The Caveat: Why I worried about Lubricants (MoS₂)
I originally pushed for MoS₂ (Solid Lubricant) because if we succeed in making the brush *too* hydrophobic (via Binder + Coating), we lose the water lubrication film.

**However, we can manage this risk without adding MoS₂ to the mix:**
*   **Strategy:** We will rely on the **Silver (Ag)** content (which acts as a lubricant) and the natural lubricity of the **Graphite**.
*   **Safety Net:** If the Friction Coefficient ($\mu$) spikes during testing, we can address it via **Impregnation** (Variable 4). We can select an impregnating oil that provides *both* hydrophobicity AND lubrication, rather than adding dry MoS₂ powder.

## Conclusion & Recommendation
I support the strategic pivot. It saves us from requalifying the electrical rating (by keeping Ag % constant) and avoids contaminating our mixers with MoS₂ powder.

**Recommendation:** Proceed with the **Pipeline 8.0** defined by your 4 parameters.
1.  Binder
2.  Pressure
3.  Temperature
4.  Impregnation
