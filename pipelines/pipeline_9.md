# Pipeline Version 9.0 (The Operational Master Plan)

**Company:** Morgan Advanced Materials (Electrical Carbon Division)
**Project:** MG1157-NextGen
**Status:** **READY FOR LAB EXECUTION**
**Documentation:** Includes Technical Appendices A-D.

---

## 1. Strategic Objective
To eliminate swelling in the MG1157 brush by optimizing the **Manufacturing Process Window** rather than reformulating the base material. We will execute a split-stream investigation across 4 process levers.

## 2. The Execution Tracks (See Appendices)

### Track 1: Binder Chemistry (The "Sponge" Control)
*   **Goal:** Replace hydrophilic Phenolic with hydrophobic alternatives.
*   **Protocol:** **[Appendix A: pipeline_9A.md]**
*   **Key Variable:** Viscosity-matched mixing of Pitch (110°C) vs. Furan (Acid Cat).

### Track 2: Mechanical Densification
*   **Goal:** Reduce open pore volume via Isostatic Pressing.
*   **Protocol:** **[Appendix B: pipeline_9B.md]**
*   **Key Variable:** Pressure Ramp (150-350 MPa) with **Controlled Decompression (<2 MPa/s)** to prevent lamination.

### Track 3: Thermal Structure
*   **Goal:** Close microporosity via high-temp sintering.
*   **Protocol:** **[Appendix C: pipeline_9C.md]**
*   **Key Variable:** Peak Temp (800-1100°C) under **<50ppm O₂** atmosphere.

### Track 4: Post-Process Sealing (Impregnation)
*   **Goal:** Seal remaining pores and add lubrication.
*   **Protocol:** **[Appendix D: pipeline_9D.md]**
*   **Key Variable:** VPI Cycle (+5 bar injection) using Silane or PFPE Oil.

---

## 3. The Validation Gates

### Gate 1: The "Swelling" Test (Accelerated)
*   **Method:** Hydrothermal Autoclave (120°C, 2 bar, 48h).
*   **Metric:** Anisotropic Expansion ($Z_{\perp}$).
*   **Pass Criteria:** $< 0.1\%$.

### Gate 2: The "Tribology" Test (Safety)
*   **Method:** Pin-on-Disc (20 A/cm²).
*   **Metric:** Friction Coefficient ($\mu$).
*   **Pass Criteria:** $\mu < 0.25$.
    *   *Contingency:* If $\mu > 0.25$, mandatory switch to **PFPE Oil Impregnation** (Appendix D).

A set of **3D Process Windows** (Pressure vs. Temp vs. Swelling) for each Binder type, overlaid with the Tribological Safe Zone.
